Smart Gas Leakage Detection

Industrial & Domestic Safety Alert System

Industry Level Use-Case

In massive industrial environments or commercial kitchens where gas is used extensively across multiple locations, an unnoticed gas leak is a ticking time bomb. This project is built to actively monitor these environments.

If a leak occurs, the system instantly detects the gas concentration. Without waiting for human intervention, it immediately triggers an A/C Gas Solenoid to cut off the main gas line, activates loud Buzzer warnings, displays the status on an LCD, and dispatches an SMS Alert (via SIM800L/900A) detailing the exact leak occurrence and time.

Aim of the Project

The main aim of this project is to avoid the accidents and deaths caused by gas leakage explosions in houses, hotels, and industries. Domestically and industrially, we use natural gas for burning purposes. If this gas is leaked and not sensed in time, it may lead to a fatal disaster, causing human and material loss. For this purpose, we have developed this "GAS LEAKAGE DETECTION SYSTEM".

Objective

The objective of this project is to detect any leakage of LPG/CNG in cars, small scale factories, or home appliances. It will detect the leakage and will close the supply knob to stop the gas flow automatically. An alarm is also present to alert the user as soon as a leakage is found, ensuring immediate awareness and safety.

Methodology

First, we use an MQ5 sensor to check if LP gas is present in the atmosphere. The signal received from the sensor is given to the Arduino UNO circuit board as an input.

Using that input, the microcontroller processes the data according to the source code. Upon detecting a leak, it triggers multiple outputs: it sends a signal to the Relay module to energize a solenoid/servo motor which shuts off the gas pipe faucet. Simultaneously, a speaker (buzzer) sounds an alarm to alert users nearby, and the SIM-900A module sends an SMS message directly to the registered mobile phone.

Hardware Components

  • Arduino Uno (Main Controller)
  • MQ5 Gas Sensor
  • LCD Display with I2C
  • Buzzer & LED Indicators
  • SIM Module 900A
  • Gas Solenoid (A/C)
  • Relay Module
  • Power Supply

MQ Sensor Identification

While this project primarily utilizes the MQ-5 sensor, here is the standard categorization of MQ series sensors used for different industrial gas detections:

Sensor Model Target Gases Detected
MQ-2 Methane, Butane, LPG, Smoke (General Combustible)
MQ-3 Alcohol, Ethanol, Smoke
MQ-4 Methane (CH4), Compressed Natural Gas (CNG)
MQ-5 (Used) Natural gas, LPG (High Sensitivity)

Future Implementations

Transitioning from a single standalone unit to a Master-Slave architecture. A central device will control multiple sub-devices across a facility. The system will feature real-time monitoring connected to a Web Dashboard, and incorporate even more advanced warning alert methodologies for enhanced industrial safety.

Conclusion

The design of a sensor-based automatic gas leakage detector with an alert and control system has been proposed and discussed in this project. This is a low-cost, low-power, lightweight, portable, safe, user-friendly, efficient, multi-featured, and simple system device for detecting gas.

Gas leakage detection will not only provide us with significance in the health department but it will also lead to raise our economy, because when gas leaks it not only contaminates the atmosphere but also wastage of gases will hurt our economy. In the open literatures it is noticed that much work has not been done for a smart gas detection system. In the future, more advanced features will be integrated with this system which will provide users with more safety and relaxation. The proliferation of handheld devices has led to developments in the field of smart gas sensors, which has considerably widened their scope of application. The need for ensuring safety in workplaces is expected to be the key driving force for the market over the coming years.
