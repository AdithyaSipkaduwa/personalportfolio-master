NATIONAL DEFENSE & INFRASTRUCTURE PROJECT

AI-Powered Thermal Locomotive Elephant Detection System

Computer Vision-Driven Early Warning Matrix Designed for Sri Lanka Railways

National Mission & Institutional Origins

This critically vital national infrastructure asset was commissioned by the Arthur C. Clarke Institute for Modern Technologies (ACCIMT) upon direct operational directives and institutional requests from the Department of Sri Lanka Railways and its oversight Ministry. The project addresses a pressing crisis along the intense Colombo-Batticaloa transit corridor: catastrophic collisions involving the localized "Meen Gaya" express train and wild elephants roaming migration tracks.

These destructive collisions consistently transpire during late-night schedules, disproportionately impacting the Class S13 Diesel Multiple Unit (DMU) power sets. Dense midnight fog and absolute wilderness darkness severely limit locomotive operators' visual ranges, rendering giant obstructions invisible until physical impact is unavoidable. This resulting crisis frequently leads to fatal wildlife loss, severe destruction of costly locomotive engines and commuter cabins, operator casualties, and structural destruction of the regional rail tracks.

Long-Range Thermal Vision & AI Integration

To overcome absolute darkness and blinding night fog, the system integrates a high-performance military-grade **Thermal Imaging Camera** boasting an effective forward scanning threshold of **up to 1 Kilometer**. Thermal vision bypasses atmospheric interference completely, affording locomotive operators the vital stopping distance and reaction time required to decelerate heavy compositions safely.

To convert raw infrared video telemetry into actionable data, advanced **Computer Vision and Machine Learning (ML) techniques** were utilized. **YOLO (You Only Look Once)** object detection models were customized and trained specifically to identify multi-angle elephant profiles instantly within heavy foliage or open tracks, suppressing false positives under demanding field conditions.

Prototyping & ACCIMT Hardware R&D

During my professional tenure as a research engineering contributor within the **Communication Division at ACCIMT**, under the direct mentorship and guidance of Project Lead & Research Engineer Mr. Jayakamal (OUSL), we built and deployed the operational system prototype. Core tasks focused on raw telemetry collection and rugged field deployment infrastructure development.

Direct locomotive mounting of sensitive thermal equipment is impossible due to harsh environment exposure, heavy rain, and jarring mechanical vibrations capable of destroying delicate sensors. To counter this, we designed an armored environmental protective housing containing an integrated stabilization **Gimbal** assembly and an automated protective shutter mechanism.

Electro-Mechanical Hardware & Prototype Assembly

The industrial execution of the automated protective closure box, built utilizing CNC-milled systems, precise stepper motor drives, and robust mechanical arm linkages to automate shelter deployments during inclement weather.

Locomotive Integration & Field Telemetry

An on-board electronic **Rain Sensor** actively monitors ambient moisture levels to trigger the automated closure assembly during heavy rain. The complete internal payload—including the stepper motor driver modules, core control circuits, thermal sensor core, and robotic gimbal stabilization units—is shielded inside the rugged environmental chassis.

Heavy-duty data links and power routing assemblies feed directly from the external chassis into the internal pilot cabin. To ensure seamless operation, our power regulation matrix directly adapts the locomotive engine's native **110V DC traction supply voltage** down to logic-level requirements.

Current System Boundaries

Despite exceptional structural performance, the fixed axial positioning of the optical chassis restricts effective scanning angles when traversing tight or wide-radius railway curves. To safeguard industrial intellectual property and align with strict **ACCIMT institutional rules and confidentiality regulations**, advanced structural schema and specific internal circuit metrics are strategically omitted from this documentation.

Deployment Success

Developed with extreme urgency over a rapid, intense **4-month timeline** to satisfy critical national safety concerns, this system successfully transitioned from R&D into functional service. The Department of Sri Lanka Railways currently utilizes the platform to safeguard equipment and preserve wildlife along high-risk migration sectors.

Future Horizons: Integrated Radar Matrix

To expand situational awareness beyond standard optical paths, active long-term research is underway at ACCIMT to develop and integrate an auxiliary **Radar Detection Layer**. Adding radar will enable the platform to map three-dimensional obstruction spaces surrounding the train track simultaneously. While processing radar data alongside thermal imaging requires deep processing iterations, this layer is actively progressing through experimental laboratory testing phases.
